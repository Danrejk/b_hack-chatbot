# Regional Alerts and Shelter Information: Lübeck, Germany

This document contains example curated civil-protection information for
Lübeck, Germany, used to ground the war_crisis subagent's answers. This is
seed/demo content, not a live feed - a production version of this feature
would refresh this document (or its equivalent) from an official source.

## Official warning channels

- NINA (Notfall-Informations- und Nachrichten-App), the German federal
  civil protection app, is the primary official channel for air-raid,
  chemical, and disaster warnings in Lübeck and the surrounding
  Schleswig-Holstein region.
- Siren signals: a continuous one-minute tone means "danger - seek
  information and shelter"; an up-and-down wailing tone means "all clear."
- Local authority: Hansestadt Lübeck civil protection office coordinates
  shelter designations and evacuation orders for the city.

## Known public shelter / reinforced locations near Lübeck city center

- Underground car parks in the city center (e.g. near An der Untertrave)
  offer below-ground reinforced space if no dedicated shelter is reachable.
- Solid-basement buildings (brick/concrete pre-war construction common in
  the Altstadt) are generally safer than modern glass-fronted buildings.
- Lübeck Hauptbahnhof (main station) has below-ground passage areas that
  can serve as temporary reinforced shelter if no better option is nearby.

## General regional advisory

- In the absence of an active official alert, the standing guidance for
  the Lübeck region is: know your nearest solid-basement building, keep a
  charged phone with the NINA app installed, and treat any siren as
  requiring immediate shelter-seeking until an official all-clear is
  confirmed.
